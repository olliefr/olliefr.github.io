There is a handful of approaches one can take to solve this problem.

  1. Run trials, each of tosses. Count how many trials scored heads. This is the most direct approach, but how does one justify the chosen number of trials? This approach is also most computationally expensive, but as we will see it has its values as it is very easy to implement and it provides a good estimate for comparison with other methods.

  3. A classic pencil and paper approach could use a normal approximation based on the central limit theorem. Statistical tables will be required to avoid any computer or calculator use. Conclusions and Meditations

Despite my education in mathematical modelling, I still get excited when a crude simulation turns out to be helpful for guiding more theoretical work. Such was the case this time as well – the simple simulation as the first option has prevented me from making an order-of-magnitude sized error in my theoretical derivations later.
